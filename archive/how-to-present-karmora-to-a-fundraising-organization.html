How to Present Karmora to a Fundraising Organization

Karmora FREE Fundraising Webstores are a great way for any organization to raise money for a worthwhile cause.  With over 1,500 stores representing more than one million products to choose from any donor base will find products they already buy online at the same exact price, and will love the added benefit that it is raising money for causes they support all year long! 

Kelly is a Karmora member that has found a particular niche building his Karmora Community of shoppers by offering FREE Karmora Webstores to fundraising organizations.  Let’s watch as Kelly presents Karmora as a fundraising option to Susan, Booster Club President, of his son’s high school football team.

After a Booster Club Meeting Kelly sparks up a conversation with Susan…

Hey Susan! 

Hey Kelly!  How ya doin?

Doing Great Susan!  Really excited about the season!

Me too!

I’ve been meaning to talk to you about a new fundraising opportunity that is completely free to the Booster Club.  It’s new and can earn the Booster Club money all year long. 

We already have fundraisers that have been working pretty well Kelly.  I am not sure we can ask our parents to do much more.

Yeah, I know… but this is something completely different.  It won’t cost the Club anything and even better, our parents won’t be asked to buy anything they aren’t already buying or spend anything more than they are already spending.  Yet, the Club can make money all year long!

OK… you’ve got my attention Kelly.  What is it?

Have you heard about Karmora?

No.  What is Karmora?

It is new Website where fundraising organizations can get FREE Fundraising Webstores pre-loaded with more than 1,500 stores that our parents are already shopping at, and I am talking about stores like Amazon, Best Buy, Target, Walmart, Macy’s… you name it… they got it… and every time our parents make a purchase the Club will earn a commission up to 40%.

Shut the front door!  If we are going to earn 40% then the prices have to be jacked up somewhere!

That’s what I thought too, but I can prove to you that is not the case.  I have tested it.  It is 100% for real and the prices are exactly the same as if you went to Macy’s online directly.

Sounds too good to be true!

Again… I thought the same thing when it was presented to me.  Not only is it NOT too good to be true, I purchased my own Webstore and we shop on it all the time!

I thought you said it was free?

It is Free, for approved fundraising organizations.  Individuals like me pay a monthly membership.  All you have to do is apply online through my Webstore and once they review your application and verify that you are a legitimate club or charity you will receive an email giving you the link to the Club’s new, and FREE, Fundraising Webstore.

Where can I see one of these FREE Fundraising Webstores?

When you have a moment I’d be happy to share my Wesbtore with you.  The Club’s Free Fundraising Webstore will be exactly like mine.  Here is my card.  Please review the “Raise It” page and let’s chat tomorrow night.  As you can see on my card my Webstore address is www.karmorakelly.com.

Sounds great.  I’ll take a look and give you a call.

Susan calls Kelly back after she gets home…

Hello Susan!

Hello Kelly!  I just checked out your Karmora Webstore and it is really nice.  I watched the Fundraising hand drawing video thingy on your Raise It page.   I watched it like four times.  Really cool and informative.  But, I have some questions.

I thought you might have some questions.  How can I help?

Well, for starters, how does Karmora know who buys what, and how will they know to pay the Club a commission when our parents make purchases?

Really good question Susan.  The short answer is that whenever a parent clicks on a store on the Fundraising Webstore it places a cookie on their computer letting Karmora and the advertisers know where the shopper came from, which in this case would be the Booster Club Webstore.  And when one of our parents makes a purchase we will get credit for the sale and receive a commission.

Wow… that is really great.  So… our parents will have all of these stores to shop on at exactly the same price as if they went to the stores themselves and when they make a purchase we get paid a commission? 

Exactly!

And we will get paid 40%?  That’s crazy!

Well… the Club won’t get 40% on all stores.  The commission amounts vary by store and sometimes by product.  You can check the commission rates by clicking View All Stores on any Webstore and there is a complete commission matrix in your back office.  Most of the major stores are between 2% and 20% but every dollar will add up if we can get our parents to shop with the Club.  And if we market it properly the Club can raise money all year long!

That makes sense.  So… what’s in this for you.

Another good question.  I will receive 20% of the total commission earned on all Webstores that join through my Webstore.  Whether it is a Free Fundraising Webstore or signing up a friend or family member I am building a community of shoppers where I earn commissions on purchases that are made every day.

So the club gets 80% of the commission and you get 20% for referring us to Karmora.  And, when we fill out the application and are approved we will then be in your Karmora Community of shoppers?

Exactly.  However, if me making money on this would be a problem, I would be happy to donate my commission back to the Club.

Oh Kelly, that wouldn’t be necessary.

Thanks Susan, but there is one caveat…

I knew there had to be a catch somewhere.  There is always a catch.

Hahaha… No catch!  I promise.  Let’s say that one of our parents decide that they want their own Karmora Webstore…  The cool thing with Karmora is that when they join from the Club’s Webstore they will be added to the Club’s Community of shoppers and the club will receive $5 per month of their membership fee and 20% of all sales made on their Webstore.  So the Club won’t lose anything if they join… you actually potentially gain $60 a year per parent plus 20% of all commissions.  It is a great fundraising platform!

That is awesome, but it sounds like multi-level marketing and I do not think the Board would approve it.

This is not multi-level marketing.  It is single level or more appropriately direct sales.  The Club would be a member of my shopping community, but if one of the parents were to join Karmora from the Club’s Webstore I am not compensated.  If I were, then that would be multi-level.

That makes sense.  But how will we get our parents to know about our store.

Easy… we place a link to the Webstore on the Club Website.  We place ads in all of the programs for the games.  We send emails designed by Karmora in the back office to our parents for holidays and general information.  We remind parents at all meetings.  The list is endless, but we don’t want to overdo it. 

I see.  So how are we paid?

You have a whole host of tools in your back office including an eWallet where Karmora transfers the commissions.  It takes 60-90 days to ramp up, but once it gets going you it can be quite profitable and every dollar counts right?!

Sounds like they got it all figured out!

Yep… and you will have access to their Fundraising Team during normal business hours, and of course I will be here to help you.

Ok… I’m sold.  Can you present it to the Board on Wednesday evening at 8:00?

I’d love to.

Kelly made the presentation to the Booster Club Board and they asked pretty much the same questions. The Board approved Kelly taking the application from his Webstore and the Booster Club was approved for a FREE Fundraising Webstore. The Booster Club and its 140 families between Freshman, JV and Varsity football are now a part of Kelly’s Karmora Community of shoppers! Way to go Kelly!

The end… Or is it just the beginning?
